The first practical videotape recorder, the Ampex VRX-1000, was unveiled on April 14, 1956, at the NARTB Convention in Chicago, revolutionizing television broadcasting. Developed by Ampex in Redwood City, California, this 2-inch quadruplex machine allowed networks to record and delay broadcasts instantly, replacing the inferior "kinescope" film method.
